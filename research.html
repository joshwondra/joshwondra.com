Overview

The focus of my research is on empathy—feeling what others feel—and sympathy—feeling concern for others who are in need.


How Do We Feel What Others Feel?

Theories of empathy try to find special processes that explain how we could feel emotions that are similar to what others feel and are disconnected from most knowledge from emotion theories. Nevertheless, vicarious emotions are still emotions and should be subject to typical emotional processes. Currently, I am trying to connect empathy and emotion research by examining how cognitive appraisals—how we interpret what is happening in a situation—determine vicarious emotional experiences the same way that they determine firsthand emotional experiences.


Feeling Concern for Others

I am researching the cognitive appraisals that characterize sympathy, compassion, pity, and tenderness to better understand whether and how they differ from each other. I hope that this line of research will help us understand why there are limits to sympathy, such as a lack of sympathy for out-group members.
